An excerpt from one of her pieces of writing on rape culture:

"Sexual violence in the west is fundamentally a problem of masculinity - a manifestation of the phenomenon that gender studies conceptualizes as 'toxic masculinity." - Jeremy Posadas toxic masculinity refers to the stereotypical, socially-constructed attitudes that describe the masculine gender role as violent, unemotional, and sexually aggressive. Toxic masculinity places men into a box but what happens if they try to leave that box? They are shamed, persecuted, told that they are not manly enough, not aggressive, not dominant, told that they are not fit to be men because they clearly don't wear the pants.  They are bludgeoned with comments like "Man up." Men trying to exert their dominance, trying to prove that they are worthy of the label man and sometimes this manifests itself in one of the most horrific crimes that can be committed against a woman: rape.  Toxic masculinity, therefore, does not only affect men but women as well as it leads to a society where women are victimised and constantly have their rights violated and ignored by the men negatively affected by toxic masculinity within the society.

Justin Magidan

Justin Magidan is a brave survivor of violence whom was willing to share his testimony for the benefit of this website.

His Testimony-

"For several years of my life I went through mental, physical and emotional abuse. It was a very hard time for me and I considered ending my life on many occasions but I couldn’t bring myself to do it. I felt trapped and I didn’t know how to get out until my grandmother took me out of the situation and introduced me to God and the catholic church. From there my life took a turn for the better and I understood the amazing grace of God and how he used ordinary people to do fantastic things for him. I decided to use my story in order to help persons going through the same thing but are afraid to speak out. Through church I met my brother and his ministry Beautifully Broken."

His attire represents his brand 'Beautifully Broken' This is a ministry geared towards providing light and hope to the world specifically towards the youth of the island.

    I have taken the liberty to give a brief overview of his ministry, in hopes of it inspiring others.


    Website Information Beautifully Broken Ministry Port-Of-Spain 21/08/2020 1 (868) 746-1374

  • Overview of Ministry-
    • Beautifully Broken Ministry is a ministry geared towards providing light and hope to the world more specifically youth. Youth is the future of the nation and so we should seek to build them accordingly. Beautifully Broken Ministry is one in which seeks to address various pressing issues of the current generation such as abuse, depression and anxiety, drug and substance abuse, gangs, self-esteem issues and more and to provide alternative measures to the youth that are feeling hopeless and helpless.
  • Vision-our vision is to become the most influential, youth driven ministry in our island and by extension the world.
  • Mission- to change lives with the incorporation our theme “Transformation through Testimony and Teaching.”
  • Tag line- “to change the world by touching one soul at a time.”
  • Speakers- Due to the very diverse dynamics of the team, we currently have persons who travel around the island sharing their testimonies to various groups, organizations and churches addressing different topics such as those listed above.
  • Annual Easter Concerts- these concerts are ran, organized, executed and performed by various youths and youth groups. This concert is geared towards the theme of resurrection in which the testimonies of various young persons would shed light on what it was like to be “dead” and now “alive” in Christ. It also seeks to bring exposure and to provide and outlet for youths to showcase their various gifts and talents such as dance, drama, music and the arts.
  • Programs and seminars- with the partnerships of other organizations we have developed programs to aid in our mission. Programs that meet the needs and concerns of the youth in our generation and aid in bringing about different perspectives and outlooks of the situations they are constantly challenged by.
  • School and childcare program- the ministry has recently took on the responsibility of housing and supporting a school for abandoned and neglected children where we seek to improve the lives of these said youths but granting them the opportunity to have a better start at life to which they were initially given.
